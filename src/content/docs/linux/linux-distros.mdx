Linux is one kernel and a thousand distributions, but the lineage is short. Almost every distro you'll meet descends from one of a handful of roots. Hover any card, and scroll to the bottom for the whole branching tree.

## Thirty years of distros, branched

UNIX and GNU sit above the timeline; the Linux kernel (1991) is the trunk. Every card above appears on the diagram at its first release year, with edges back to parents — including historical RPM lines (Mandrake → Mandriva → Mageia / OpenMandriva), CentOS&nbsp;Linux → CentOS&nbsp;Stream, and Debian- and Ubuntu-based desktops (Zorin, elementary, MX). Defunct distros are still drawn so the forks make sense.
