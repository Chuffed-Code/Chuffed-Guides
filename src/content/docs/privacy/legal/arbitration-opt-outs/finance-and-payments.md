### Charles Schwab

**✗ No opt-out available** · *Deadline: N/A — FINRA Required*

Schwab's brokerage agreements contain mandatory predispute arbitration. No opt-out. But FINRA arbitration is a regulated process with real consumer protections — significantly better than corporate arbitration.

**What it covers:** All disputes: (i) this agreement or any other agreement with Schwab, (ii) your account or any other Schwab account or services, (iii) transactions, or (iv) any aspect of the relationship with Schwab, its parent, subsidiaries, affiliates, officers, directors, employees, agents, or service providers. Binding on heirs, successors, and court-appointed trustees.

**How to opt out:**

- Schwab does NOT offer an opt-out — arbitration is a condition of opening any brokerage account.
- This is standard across ALL FINRA-member broker-dealers (Fidelity, Vanguard, E*TRADE, Merrill, etc.).
- FINRA rules do not actually require firms to use arbitration — but nearly all choose to.
- HOWEVER: FINRA arbitration has real consumer protections that corporate arbitration lacks:
- → YOU get a say in selecting arbitrators (both sides rank candidates from FINRA's random list).
- → Customer cases use 'public' arbitrators (not industry insiders) — you can request an all-public panel.
- → FINRA publishes all arbitration awards publicly (unlike corporate arbitration which is secret).
- → You can search every historical award free at finra.org/arbitration-mediation/arbitration-awards.
- → FINRA prohibits firms from using class action waivers to block court class actions (FINRA Rule 2268).
- → You have 6 years from misconduct to file (FINRA Rule 12206).
- → Cases typically resolve in 12–16 months (faster than most courts).
- For former TD Ameritrade customers: the only 'opt-out' during migration was liquidating or transferring assets before the deadline.
- To file: go to finra.org/arbitration-mediation/file-claim or mail to FINRA Dispute Resolution Services, 200 Liberty Street, New York, NY 10281.

**Contact:** FINRA Dispute Resolution: finra.org/arbitration-mediation  |  (800) 700-7065  |  Schwab: schwab.com/legal

:::note
IMPORTANT DISTINCTION: FINRA arbitration is NOT the same as corporate forced arbitration (Disney, Uber, etc.). FINRA is a regulated SRO with published rules, mandatory arbitrator disclosure reports, public award databases, and consumer protections. In 2025, a FINRA panel awarded investors nearly $350K including punitive damages against Schwab in a cherry-picking fraud case. Schwab has 312 disclosures on its FINRA BrokerCheck record and paid $187M in a 2022 SEC settlement for robo-advisor misconduct. The system isn't perfect, but it's meaningfully better than private corporate arbitration. Filing fees range from $50–$1,575 depending on claim size. Schwab pays most arbitration costs for smaller claims.
:::

### Coinbase

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Coinbase updated its arbitration clause in March/May 2025, adding JAMS as a fallback forum and lowering the Batch arbitration threshold from 100 to 25 claimants. 30-day email opt-out available.

**What it covers:** All disputes with Coinbase, Inc. and affiliates (Coinbase Crypto Services, Toshi Holdings, etc.) related to platform use, account access, crypto holdings, and trading. Class action and jury trial waivers. Includes disputes that arose BEFORE the current terms.

**How to opt out:**

- Email your opt-out to Coinbase within 30 days of accepting the updated User Agreement.
- Check coinbase.com/legal/user_agreement/united_states for the current opt-out email address (historically arbitration-opt-out@coinbase.com, but verify in the current Appendix 5).
- Include: your full name, the email address associated with your Coinbase account, and a clear statement of intent to opt out of binding arbitration.
- Deadline: 30 days from accepting the new terms (new users: from signup; existing users: from the May 15, 2025 effective date, if not previously opted out).
- Pre-arbitration Formal Complaint Process (Section 7.2) is still required even if you opt out — you must submit Coinbase's complaint form and wait 45 business days before filing any claim.
- Small claims court is preserved for qualifying claims.
- Keep proof of the email and any reply.

**Contact:** See Appendix 5 of the current User Agreement at coinbase.com/legal/user_agreement/united_states

:::note
The March 2025 update was a meaningful downgrade: the batch-arbitration threshold dropped from 100 claimants to 25, meaning disputes get consolidated sooner — and JAMS (which is more expensive than AAA) can now be used. Coinbase courts have consistently enforced these clauses (Aggarwal v. Coinbase, Bielski v. Coinbase). Hacked-account and SIM-swap victims have been compelled to arbitrate rather than sue. If you hold meaningful crypto on Coinbase, opting out preserves your right to pursue individual court claims or join class actions over platform-wide issues (outages, security failures, etc.). The December 2025 TOS update did NOT change dispute resolution, but did add a Token Sale Platform and separate U.S. equities trading through Coinbase Capital Markets Corp.

### Fidelity

**✗ No opt-out available** · *Deadline: N/A — FINRA Required*

Fidelity's agreements contain predispute arbitration via FINRA. No opt-out. Same regulated FINRA process as Schwab — not the same as corporate forced arbitration.

**What it covers:** All controversies between you and Fidelity concerning any subject matter, issue, or circumstance — including but not limited to controversies concerning any account, transaction, or the relationship. Customer cases with claims over $100K get a three-arbitrator panel; you can request that ALL three be 'public' (non-industry) arbitrators. FINRA arbitrators take an oath of neutrality and must disclose all potential conflicts. Not perfect, but far more structured and fair than the private arbitration used by Disney, Uber, or your credit card company.

### Northwest Registered Agent

**✗ No opt-out available** · *Deadline: N/A*

Northwest Registered Agent uses an asymmetric clause — THEY reserve the sole right to force arbitration. Class action waiver. 60-day informal dispute period required first. Washington jurisdiction.

**What it covers:** All disputes with Northwest Registered Agent (the LLC formation and registered agent service). Governed by Washington State law. Claims must be filed within 1 year.

**Contact:** Legal Department, Northwest Registered Agent, 522 W Riverside Ave Ste N, Spokane, WA 99201  |  legal@northwestregisteredagent.com

:::note
Asymmetric arbitration clauses like this are increasingly being challenged as unconscionable (because the consumer can't force arbitration but the company can), though courts have generally upheld them under the FAA. If you use Northwest as your registered agent, also be aware that their mail service has fees for document retrieval after account closure, and they've reportedly refused to forward mail to canceled-service customers without the $50/document fee.
