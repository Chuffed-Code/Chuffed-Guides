:::caution[Before you start]
Most opt-out windows are only **30 days** from when you created your account or agreed to updated terms. If you signed up recently, this is time-critical. See the [overview](../) for the universal opt-out letter template and general guidance.
:::

### Disney+ / Hulu / ESPN+

**✉️ Mail opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Forced arbitration clause survives account cancellation — potentially binds you for life. Added after the infamous Piccolo wrongful death case backlash.

**What it covers:** All disputes with Disney and its affiliates, including theme parks, restaurants, and streaming. Class action waiver included. Survives termination of your subscription.

**How to opt out:**

- Write a letter including: your name, address, account email, and a clear statement opting out of both arbitration AND the class action waiver.
- Mail to: Disney Opt-Out, P.O. Box 11565, Burbank, California 91510
- Must be received within 30 days of first agreeing to terms.
- Email opt-outs are explicitly NOT accepted — must be physical mail.
- Keep a copy + proof of mailing (certified mail recommended).

**Contact:** Disney Opt-Out, P.O. Box 11565, Burbank, CA 91510

:::note
Disney tried to use this clause to force a wrongful death lawsuit into arbitration in 2024 (the Piccolo case). They backed down after massive public backlash, but the clause remains. As of Feb 2025, it extends to Hulu and ESPN+ too.
:::

### Netflix

**✗ No opt-out available** · *Deadline: N/A*

Netflix has a binding arbitration clause with NO opt-out provision for consumers. You agree by using the service.

**What it covers:** All disputes arising from the Netflix service, website, and Terms of Use. Class action waiver included. Survives termination.

**How to opt out:**

- Netflix does NOT currently offer an opt-out for its arbitration clause.
- You can still file in small claims court for qualifying disputes.
- To initiate arbitration: send written Notice to General Counsel, Netflix, Inc., 100 Winchester Circle, Los Gatos, CA 95032-1815.

**Contact:** General Counsel, Netflix, Inc., 100 Winchester Circle, Los Gatos, CA 95032-1815

:::note
Netflix was one of the early adopters of forced arbitration (since 2012). Unlike many competitors, they provide no opt-out window. Your only alternatives are small claims court or choosing not to subscribe.
:::

### Spotify

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days (for TOS changes)*

Spotify allows opt-out of material changes to its arbitration agreement within 30 days of notification, but the base arbitration clause has a staged mediation opt-out process.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to the Spotify service, including billing, content, and account issues. Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- For material changes to arbitration terms: Send a personally signed written notice to Spotify within 30 days of being notified.
- Email: tounotice@spotify.com
- Include: your name, address, Spotify account email, phone number, and username.
- Must be sent from your personal email, not by an agent or attorney.
- Include a statement that you wish to reject the change.
- Note: opting out of a change does NOT opt you out of arbitration entirely — only reverts to the prior version.

**Contact:** tounotice@spotify.com

:::note
Spotify's arbitration terms include a complex staged mediation process. Dispute notices go to tounotice@spotify.com. For initial disputes, send a written Notice describing the claim and relief sought.
:::
