### AT&T

**✉️ Mail opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

AT&T was the company behind the landmark Supreme Court case (AT&T v. Concepcion, 2011) that enabled mass adoption of forced arbitration.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to AT&T services. The clause AT&T won in the Supreme Court effectively set the precedent for every other company on this list.

**How to opt out:**

- Send written notice to AT&T within 30 days of activating service.
- Include: your name, address, and account number.
- Mail to the address in your current AT&T service agreement.
- Check your specific agreement — AT&T has different terms for wireless, internet, and TV services.

**Contact:** See your specific AT&T service agreement for current opt-out address

:::note
AT&T v. Concepcion (2011) is the Supreme Court case that supercharged forced arbitration across all industries. The ruling upheld AT&T's arbitration clause and its class action waiver, opening the floodgates for every company on this list.
:::

### Comcast / Xfinity

**✓ Has opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Comcast offers one of the most consumer-friendly opt-out processes with a dedicated online form.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to Xfinity services including internet, TV, voice, and mobile. Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- Online form (easiest): Visit xfinity.com/arbitrationoptout
- For Xfinity Mobile: xfinity.com/arbitrationoptout/mobile
- For web services (non-customers): xfinity.com/webarboptout
- OR mail to: Comcast, 1701 John F. Kennedy Blvd., Philadelphia, PA 19103-2838, ATTN: Legal Department/Arbitration
- Include: your name, address, account number (or phone number if not a subscriber), and a clear statement opting out.
- Must be within 30 days of first service activation or website use.
- If you previously opted out, you do NOT need to do so again. But you must separately opt out for each account.

**Contact:** xfinity.com/arbitrationoptout  |  Comcast, 1701 JFK Blvd., Philadelphia, PA 19103-2838, ATTN: Legal Dept/Arbitration

:::note
Comcast explicitly states that opting out has no adverse effect on your relationship or services. One of the few major companies with a dedicated online opt-out form.
:::

### Ripple Fiber

**✗ No opt-out available** · *Deadline: Check agreement*

Ripple Fiber is a smaller regional ISP. Check your specific service agreement for any arbitration provisions.

**What it covers:** Check your service agreement.

**How to opt out:**

- Check your Ripple Fiber service agreement for any arbitration clause.
- Smaller ISPs may or may not include forced arbitration.
- If they do, look for an opt-out provision — many ISPs model their terms after larger providers.
- Contact Ripple Fiber customer service to request a copy of your agreement if you don't have one.

**Contact:** Contact Ripple Fiber customer service

:::note
Smaller and regional ISPs vary widely in their approach to arbitration. Some copy the playbook of large ISPs like Comcast, others don't include it at all. The only way to know is to read your specific agreement.
:::

### Visible (Verizon)

**✓ Has opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Visible is a Verizon sub-brand. Check Visible's specific Customer Agreement for arbitration terms — Verizon brands typically include arbitration with a 30-day opt-out.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to your Visible wireless service.

**How to opt out:**

- Check visible.com/legal/terms for the current Customer Agreement.
- Verizon sub-brands typically offer a 30-day opt-out by written notice.
- Follow the specific instructions in the terms.
- Visible's terms may differ from Verizon's main wireless agreement.

**Contact:** See visible.com/legal for current details

:::note
As a Verizon-owned brand, Visible likely mirrors Verizon's arbitration framework but may have its own specific terms. AT&T's arbitration clause was the one that went to the Supreme Court in 2011, setting the precedent that enables all of this.
:::
