:::caution[Before you start]
Most opt-out windows are only **30 days** from when you created your account or agreed to updated terms. If you signed up recently, this is time-critical. See the [overview](../) for the universal opt-out letter template and general guidance.
:::

### DoorDash

**✉️ Mail opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

DoorDash requires a signed physical letter to opt out. Famously ordered by a judge to pay $12M in arbitration fees for 5,010 cases.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to earnings, tips, deactivation, injury, and classification. Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- Write a signed letter stating your intent to opt out of arbitration.
- Include: full name, address, and date.
- Mail via certified mail (keep tracking proof).
- Address: DoorDash, Inc., Attn: Legal Department, 303 2nd Street, South Tower, 8th Floor, San Francisco, CA 94107
- Must be postmarked within 30 days of accepting terms.

**Contact:** DoorDash, Inc., Attn: Legal Department, 303 2nd St, South Tower, 8th Floor, San Francisco, CA 94107

:::note
A judge famously scolded DoorDash for trying to avoid arbitrating 5,010 individual claims after the company itself had required arbitration, calling it 'irony upon irony' and 'hypocrisy.' DoorDash later switched from AAA to CPR for arbitration.
:::

### Grubhub

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Grubhub drivers have 30 days after accepting terms to opt out of arbitration.

**What it covers:** All disputes between you and Grubhub related to the platform, payments, and working conditions.

**How to opt out:**

- Opt out within 30 days of accepting terms.
- Rideshare Drivers United provides a tool at drivers-united.org to help streamline the process.
- Send written notice stating intent to opt out with your account details.

**Contact:** Check current TOS for specific email/address — changes with updates

:::note
Grubhub's arbitration clause has been in place for years. Like other gig platforms, terms update periodically and may require re-opting-out.
:::

### Instacart

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Instacart allows email opt-out within 30 days. Also allows opt-out of changes to the arbitration agreement.

**What it covers:** All disputes with Instacart and its retailers. Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- Email: arbitration-opt-out@instacart.com
- Include: your first and last name, and the email associated with your account.
- State your intent to opt out.
- Must be within 30 days of first accepting terms.
- You can also reject changes to arbitration terms within 30 days of the change being effective.

**Contact:** arbitration-opt-out@instacart.com

:::note
Opting out does not affect any other part of the Terms of Service. Instacart will pay arbitration fees if you can't afford them and can't get a fee waiver from the arbitration service.
:::

### Lyft

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Lyft's TOS updated Feb 2026 with arbitration clause. Drivers and riders have 30 days to opt out via email.

**What it covers:** All disputes including deactivation, payment disputes, injury claims, and contract violations. Survives termination. Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- Send email to: arbitrationoptout@lyft.com
- Include: your full name, account email, phone number, and a statement of intent to opt out.
- Must be sent within 30 days of accepting the agreement.
- Like Uber, re-opt-out after each TOS update.
- Drivers may opt out of revisions to arbitration provisions for certain claims.

**Contact:** arbitrationoptout@lyft.com

:::note
Lyft's latest TOS is from February 2026. Arbitration administered by AAA and governed by the Federal Arbitration Act. Lyft explicitly states this agreement survives termination.
:::

### Uber (Riders & Drivers)

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Uber updates its TOS frequently. Every time you accept new terms, you must opt out AGAIN within 30 days.

**What it covers:** All disputes between you and Uber, including fares, accidents, deactivation, and safety. Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- Send an email from the address associated with your Uber account.
- Email to: optout@uber.com
- Subject line: 'Opt-Out of Arbitration'
- Include: your full legal name, phone number on your account, and city of residence.
- Must be sent within 30 days of accepting new Terms of Service.
- CRITICAL: You must re-opt-out every time Uber updates its TOS — previous opt-outs may not carry forward.

**Contact:** optout@uber.com

:::note
One driver reported receiving $9,500 in the O'Connor v. Uber settlement specifically because they had opted out of arbitration. Uber has faced over 12,500 individual arbitration demands. Re-opt-out after every TOS update.
:::
