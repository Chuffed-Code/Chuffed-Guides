:::caution[Before you start]
Most opt-out windows are only **30 days** from when you created your account or agreed to updated terms. If you signed up recently, this is time-critical. See the [overview](../) for the universal opt-out letter template and general guidance.
:::

### Discord

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Discord has one of the easiest opt-outs — just email from your account email. They explicitly encourage you to opt out if you wish.

**What it covers:** All disputes between you and Discord related to the services, including claims that arose before the current terms existed. US and Canada residents.

**How to opt out:**

- Email: arbitration-opt-out@discord.com
- Send from the email address associated with your Discord account.
- No specific template required — just state you want to opt out.
- Must be sent within 30 days of September 29, 2025 (latest TOS update) or when you first register, whichever is later.
- You will NOT be penalized in any way for opting out.
- Discord explicitly says: 'We encourage you to opt out if you wish to.'

**Contact:** arbitration-opt-out@discord.com

:::note
Discord is unusually transparent about this. They extended the original 30-day window to 90 days after user backlash in 2018, and have explicitly stated they encourage opting out. Keep a copy of your email as proof.
:::

### LinkedIn

**✗ No opt-out available** · *Deadline: N/A*

LinkedIn is owned by Microsoft. Its dispute resolution follows Microsoft's terms — binding arbitration for US users with no clear consumer opt-out.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to LinkedIn services. Governed by Microsoft's arbitration framework.

**How to opt out:**

- LinkedIn does not appear to offer a separate consumer arbitration opt-out.
- Disputes are governed by LinkedIn's User Agreement, which incorporates Microsoft-style arbitration terms.
- Small claims court remains an option.

**Contact:** See linkedin.com/legal/user-agreement

:::note
LinkedIn is a Microsoft subsidiary. Check both LinkedIn's User Agreement and Microsoft's Services Agreement for the full picture of your dispute resolution rights.
:::

### Medium

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Medium offers one of the cleanest opt-outs — a simple email to trust@medium.com within 30 days. JAMS arbitration in San Francisco (or your county of residence if you're a consumer).

**What it covers:** All disputes related to Medium's Terms or services, including privacy, data security, content, and account claims. Class and representative actions prohibited unless you opt out. 1-year filing deadline on all disputes.

**How to opt out:**

- Email: trust@medium.com
- Must be sent within 30 days of first accepting the arbitration terms.
- Include: your full name, your address, and a clear statement of intent to opt out of binding arbitration.
- If you validly opt out, disputes go to the state or federal courts of California, San Francisco County.
- Consumers (personal/family/household use) can elect to have any arbitration hearing in their own county of residence.
- Medium caps consumer filing fees at $250 and pays the rest of JAMS fees.

**Contact:** trust@medium.com

:::note
Medium's opt-out is email-based with no signature or notarization required — genuinely easy. The 1-year filing limit is aggressive; standard contract claims get 3-6 years in most states. Small claims court is also preserved for individual actions in your county. Even after opting out, the class action and representative-action waiver still applies — so you regain your right to sue individually in court, not as a class.
:::

### Meta (Facebook, Instagram, Quest)

**✉️ Mail opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Meta's supplemental terms for Meta Platforms Technologies products (Quest, etc.) include arbitration with a mail-only opt-out.

**What it covers:** All disputes arising from Meta Platforms Technologies products (Quest VR, smart glasses, etc.). Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- Send a physical letter to: Meta Platforms, Inc., ATTN: MPT Arbitration Opt-out, 1 Meta Way, Menlo Park, CA 94025
- Include: your name, username, residence address, email address, phone number, and a personally signed statement that you want to opt out.
- Must be within 30 days of first agreeing to terms.
- You can also opt out of revisions to arbitration terms within 30 days of receiving notice.

**Contact:** Meta Platforms, Inc., ATTN: MPT Arbitration Opt-out, 1 Meta Way, Menlo Park, CA 94025

:::note
Meta's main Facebook/Instagram TOS also contains arbitration provisions. The MPT terms specifically cover hardware products. Facebook has included arbitration clauses in commercial TOS and employee contracts. Check current TOS for each service.
:::

### Reddit

**✉️ Mail opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Reddit's User Agreement includes mandatory arbitration with a 30-day opt-out by mail.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to Reddit's services. Class action waiver included.

**How to opt out:**

- Check reddit.com/policies/user-agreement for the current opt-out procedure.
- Typically requires written notice within 30 days of agreeing to terms.
- Mail to the address specified in the User Agreement.
- Include your Reddit username and a clear statement of opt-out.

**Contact:** See reddit.com/policies for current mailing address

:::note
Reddit went public in 2024 and has been increasingly corporate about its legal terms. Check the current User Agreement for the latest arbitration and opt-out provisions.
:::

### Snapchat

**📧 Email opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

Snapchat offers both email and mail opt-out. 30 days from first becoming subject to the arbitration agreement.

**What it covers:** All disputes arising from or related to the Terms or use of Snapchat services. Class action waiver included (can opt out of both, or just arbitration).

**How to opt out:**

- Email: arbitration-opt-out@snap.com
- OR mail to: Snap Inc., Attn: Arbitration Opt-out, 3000 31st Street, Santa Monica, CA 90405
- Include: your name, address, Snapchat username, account email, and an unequivocal statement that you want to opt out.
- You may opt out of both arbitration AND the class action waiver together.
- Must be within 30 days of first becoming subject to the agreement.

**Contact:** arbitration-opt-out@snap.com  OR  Snap Inc., Attn: Arbitration Opt-out, 3000 31st Street, Santa Monica, CA 90405

:::note
You cannot opt out of only the class action waiver without also opting out of arbitration. But you CAN opt out of arbitration alone (class action waiver would still apply).
:::

### Substack

**✗ No opt-out available** · *Deadline: N/A*

Substack requires JAMS binding arbitration in San Francisco. Class, consolidated, and representative arbitrations are prohibited. No consumer opt-out found in current Terms.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to Substack's Terms, the Publisher Agreement, content, payments, and platform use. Applies to both readers and creators/publishers. Governed by California law.

**How to opt out:**

- Substack does NOT offer an opt-out of its arbitration clause.
- Small claims court and injunctive relief for IP are preserved as exceptions.
- Disputes with individual publishers (for content issues) are between you and the publisher — Substack disclaims involvement.
- Substack uses JAMS Streamlined Arbitration Rules, which are faster and cheaper than standard JAMS, but still require filing fees.

**Contact:** See substack.com/tos and substack.com/pa (Publisher Agreement)

:::note
Substack's position is aggressive for a publishing platform — writers depending on Substack for their livelihood have no collective mechanism to challenge platform-wide policy decisions (content moderation, payment processing changes, Notes feature rollout, Nazi-content controversies). Each creator must arbitrate individually in SF. The Publisher Agreement's arbitration clause survives termination, so even former Substack writers remain bound.
:::

### TikTok

**✉️ Mail opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

TikTok has mandatory arbitration with a 30-day mail opt-out. TikTok previously settled a $92M class action over data privacy.

**What it covers:** All disputes with TikTok/ByteDance related to the services, privacy, data collection, etc.

**How to opt out:**

- Check tiktok.com/legal/terms-of-service for current opt-out instructions.
- Typically requires written notice within 30 days of agreeing to terms.
- Mail to the address specified in the Terms of Service.
- Include your name, TikTok username, and a clear opt-out statement.

**Contact:** See tiktok.com/legal for current mailing address

:::note
TikTok settled a $92M class action over biometric data collection in 2021. Users who had NOT been locked into arbitration received payouts. This is a prime example of why opting out matters.
:::

### X (formerly Twitter)

**✉️ Mail opt-out** · *Deadline: 30 days*

X/Twitter has mandatory arbitration in its Terms of Service with a 30-day opt-out provision.

**What it covers:** All disputes related to X's services.

**How to opt out:**

- Check x.com/tos for the current opt-out procedure.
- Typically requires written notice within 30 days of agreeing to terms.
- X has updated its TOS frequently under Musk's ownership — re-check after each update.

**Contact:** See x.com/tos for current details

:::note
X's TOS has changed frequently since the Twitter acquisition. Check the current version for the latest arbitration and opt-out provisions. Some employees who opted out of arbitration during the Twitter era won rare settlements.
:::
