**Every major company with forced arbitration in their Terms of Service — and exactly how to opt out.**

:::caution[Time is critical]
Most opt-out windows are only **30 days** from the date you created your account or agreed to updated terms. After that window closes, you're locked into arbitration — potentially for life. Opting out does *not* affect your account or service. It only preserves your right to sue or join a class action if something goes wrong.
:::

:::note[Why opt out?]
Forced arbitration means you waive your right to a jury trial or class action. Disputes are handled privately by an arbitrator often selected by the company. Decisions are typically final with no appeal. Opting out costs nothing, takes minutes, and preserves your legal rights — you can still choose arbitration later if you want to.
:::

## Universal opt-out letter template (for mail-based opt-outs)

```
[Your Full Name]
[Your Address]
[City, State ZIP]
[Date]

[Company Name]
[Opt-Out Address]

Re: Opt-Out of Arbitration Agreement

Dear [Company Name]:

I am writing to formally notify you that I wish to opt out of the mandatory
arbitration provision in your Terms of Service / User Agreement.

Name: [Your Full Name]
Email on Account: [your@email.com]
Username/Account #: [if applicable]
Phone: [your phone number]

I do not agree to resolve disputes through binding arbitration and wish to
preserve my right to pursue legal action in a court of law, including
participation in class action proceedings.

Sincerely,

[Your Signature]
[Your Printed Name]
```

:::tip[Use your PMB address]
If you've set up a PMB ghost address (see [the PMB guide](/privacy/identity/pmb-ghost-address/)), use it here instead of your home address. There's no reason to hand a company your real home address just to opt out of their arbitration clause.
:::
