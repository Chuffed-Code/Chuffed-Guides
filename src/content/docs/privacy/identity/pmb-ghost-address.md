**Privacy Fundamentals · Level 1 — Ghost Address Only · No license change or relocation required**

Every company, every government database, every data broker — they all know exactly where you sleep at night. A Personal Mail Box (PMB) is how you break that chain, permanently.

## Why your home address is dangerous

Your physical address is the single easiest way to find you. It's attached to everything — your bank accounts, your voter registration, your credit report. And it's being sold, leaked, and shared constantly.

- **🏠 Data brokers sell it.** Sites like Spokeo, WhitePages, and BeenVerified scrape public records and sell your home address to anyone for a few dollars. Stalkers, scammers, and angry strangers all have access.
- **📬 Your mail can be hijacked.** For $1.10, anyone can go to the USPS website and permanently redirect your home mail to a different address. They can also put your mail on hold — all without your knowledge or consent.
- **🔗 Everything links back to it.** Your bank accounts, credit reports, insurance, voter records, property tax — they all point to one place: your front door. One leak exposes everything.
- **🔍 You can't undo it easily.** Once your address is in the system, it spreads to hundreds of databases instantly. Removing it from each one is a never-ending battle — unless you replace it with something better.

## What is a Personal Mail Box (PMB)?

A PMB is a real mailing address at a physical location that functions like a residential address — but you never set foot there. It collects your mail, scans it, and forwards it wherever you want. It becomes your official address on paper, while your real home stays invisible.

```
📄 Banks, Credit Cards, etc.       →   📦 PMB in South Dakota
   (Send mail to your PMB)              (Collects & scans everything)
                                              ↓
🏡 You, at Home                    ←   📮 Local Pickup Spot
   (Invisible & untraceable)            (Near you, not your home)
```

:::tip[Key insight]
You *want* your real name attached to this PMB. The goal isn't to hide from institutions — it's to redirect them. When someone searches for you, they find a mail facility shared by thousands of people. Dead end.
:::

## PMB vs. PO Box vs. UPS Store

Not all mailboxes are created equal. Here's why a PMB is the only option that actually works for serious privacy.

| Feature | PO Box / UPS Store | PMB (Americas Mailbox) |
|---|---|---|
| Accepted by banks & credit cards | ✕ Flagged & denied | ✓ Widely accepted |
| Looks residential in databases | ✕ Instantly identified as commercial | ~ Less securitized, more accepted over time |
| Protected from USPS mail redirect | ✕ Anyone can redirect for $1.10 | ✓ Cannot be changed or forwarded by outsiders |
| Envelope scanning | ✕ Not available | ✓ Email scans of all incoming mail |
| Nationwide mail forwarding | ✕ Must visit in person | ✓ Forward anywhere, anytime |
| Government USPS forms required | ⚠ Form 1093 — creates gov record of your location | ✓ Form 1583 — links you to SD, not your real area |
| Foundation for future privacy steps | ✕ No — too easily blocked | ✓ Yes — trusts, LLCs, anonymous ownership |

:::note[Why the difference?]
Every CMRA address (including UPS stores) exists in a database that banks and governments check. They're all flagged. But PMBs used by thousands of full-time travelers are treated differently — institutions are less likely to block them because so many people legitimately use them as permanent addresses. You can't *open* new accounts with a PMB right away, but you can *change* existing accounts to it. Over time, as more institutions report it, the PMB becomes your "confirmed" address.
:::

## How to set up your ghost address

The recommended provider is Americas Mailbox (americasmailbox.com) in South Dakota. Here's exactly what to do, in order. You keep your current state, license, and life — this is just a mailing address change.

### Step 1 — Choose your plan

Go to americasmailbox.com and download the Mail Service Agreement. Choose the **Titanium Plus SuperScan Plan**.

This plan includes: a unique PMB address, mail collection & storage, forwarding to any address you choose (shipping store, hotel, etc.), and **envelope scanning** — you'll get an email with a digital image of every piece of mail that arrives, so you can decide what to forward and what to ignore.

### Step 2 — Fill out the Mail Service Agreement

Provide your **real name** and **driver's license number** on this form.

:::note
**This is intentional.** You want your true identity linked to this address. The whole strategy is to make every database, credit bureau, and data broker point HERE instead of your actual home. This is your ghost address — you're not hiding from it, you're redirecting to it.
:::

Paying with a credit card is perfectly fine. This is the one address you want openly associated with your name.

### Step 3 — Complete USPS Form 1583

This is a federal form that authorizes Americas Mailbox to receive and forward your mail. Download it from about.usps.com/forms/ps1583.pdf.

- **Box 2 — Your Name:** Use your complete legal name. Don't be vague.
- **Box 5 — Additional Names:** Include nicknames, maiden names, and at least one trust name. Even if you don't have a trust yet, listing a generic trust name here increases the odds that future trust-addressed mail will be accepted rather than returned.
- **Boxes 7a–7e — Current Address:** You can provide any current mailing address. This is never verified. Your current home address or PO Box works fine.

### Step 4 — Submit your ID

You need at least **one government photo ID** and **one secondary ID** (doesn't need a photo, just your name).

:::tip[Pro tip]
Use your **passport or passport card** as your primary ID — unlike a driver's license, it doesn't display a home address. For the second ID, a utility bill with your name works fine.
:::

### Step 5 — Get everything notarized

Some parts of the application must be signed in front of a notary public. Don't skip this — the application can be rejected without it. Most banks, UPS stores, and shipping centers offer notary services for a small fee.

### Step 6 — Submit & wait (~1 week)

Send in the completed agreement, Form 1583, your IDs, and payment. Within about a week, you'll receive a welcome packet at your current address with your new PMB number and full mailing address.

Your new address will look something like:

**514 Americas Way, PMB 143, Box Elder, SD 57719**

### Step 7 — Set up your local pickup point

Find a **local independent shipping store** a town or two away from your actual home. This is where your PMB will forward packages to. You pick them up here — never at your front door.

**Why independent stores over UPS?** Small mom-and-pop shipping stores often don't require USPS Form 1093, which means no government record of your presence in that area. Some people pay just $3.00 per package with a $20 deposit — no ID checks, no official paperwork. The store just logs your name in their own internal system. Much cheaper and more private than a UPS box.

:::danger[Critical warning]
NEVER have your PMB forward mail directly to your home. In a real case, a stalker called a PMB provider, pretended to be the client's husband, and an employee read back the home forwarding address without hesitation. Always use a commercial intermediary.
:::

### Step 8 — Test the full chain

Send a test letter to your new PMB address. Wait for the scan notification email confirming it arrived. Then request it be forwarded to your local pickup spot. Confirm you can retrieve it. Once the full chain works — PMB receives, scans, forwards, you pick up — you're operational.

### Step 9 — Manually migrate your addresses

Begin changing your mailing address **one institution at a time** — banks, brokerage firms, credit cards, insurance, and anything else that reports to credit bureaus.

**Why this matters:** As each institution reports your new South Dakota address, it spreads across credit bureaus and data mining companies like LexisNexis and CLEAR. Within about a month, your credit report and public records will show the PMB as your address. This is the goal — anyone searching for you hits a mail facility shared by thousands instead of your house.

**Hold off on:** Your current driver's license and vehicle registration. Those require additional steps and are part of a more advanced privacy plan (Level 2 — nomad residency). For now, keep those as-is.

:::caution[Do NOT file a USPS Change of Address form]
When you file a COA, the USPS feeds that data into the National Change of Address (NCOA) database, which is shared with data brokers and marketing companies. You are literally announcing your move — including both your old and new addresses — to the exact people you're trying to hide from. Instead, update each institution manually. It takes longer, but it doesn't broadcast a neat record linking your old life to your new ghost address.
:::

### Step 10 — Use your ghost address going forward

From this point on, give out your PMB address any time you would have previously given your home address or a PO Box. New accounts, subscriptions, online purchases — everything points to South Dakota now.

:::tip[The compounding effect]
The more institutions that report this address, the more "confirmed" it becomes in public databases. After several years of consistent use, you may even be able to open brand new accounts (credit cards, medical services) directly with the PMB — something that's usually blocked for commercial addresses. The key is patience and consistency.
:::

## What this actually achieves

Even without changing your license or becoming a nomad, a Level 1 ghost address fundamentally changes your privacy profile.

- **🔒 Dead-end searches.** Anyone looking you up on data brokers or people-search sites finds a mail facility in South Dakota shared by thousands. No way to find your real home from there.
- **🛡️ Mail hijack protection.** Your PMB can't be redirected, held, or forwarded through the USPS. That $1.10 attack that works on home addresses? Doesn't work on a PMB.
- **📊 Credit report cleaned.** Within weeks, your credit report starts showing the SD address instead of your home. LexisNexis, CLEAR, and similar services follow. Your home address fades from records.
- **📬 Mail visibility.** Envelope scanning means you see everything that arrives without being anywhere near it. Forward only what matters, ignore the rest.
- **🧱 Foundation built.** When you're ready for Level 2 (trusts, LLCs, nomad residency, anonymous ownership), the PMB is already in place. Everything builds on this single address.
- **⚡ Real-world tested.** This exact method has protected clients from stalkers, doxing attacks, SIM swapping attempts, and unauthorized mail redirects. It's not theoretical — it works.
